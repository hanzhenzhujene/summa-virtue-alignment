# Fortitude Closure 136-140 Notes

## Scope

This reviewed closure block covers only:

- `II-II q.136` patience
- `II-II q.137` perseverance
- `II-II q.138` the vices opposed to perseverance
- `II-II q.139` the gift of fortitude
- `II-II q.140` the precepts of fortitude

It connects to already reviewed fortitude material in `qq. 129–135` only for controlled tract synthesis.

## Normalization Decisions

- `concept.patience` remains distinct from `concept.perseverance_virtue`
- `concept.perseverance_virtue` remains distinct from earlier act-level `concept.perseverance`
- `concept.fortitude_gift` remains distinct from virtue-level `concept.fortitude`
- `concept.longanimity_fortitude` and `concept.constancy_fortitude` are modeled as nearby comparison concepts, not silent aliases of patience or perseverance

## Gift And Precept Modeling

- `corresponding_gift_of` is used for the direct relation between `fortitude_gift` and virtue-level `fortitude`
- `precept_of`, `commands_act_of`, and `forbids_opposed_vice_of` are used conservatively in `q.140`
- the closure block does not auto-link all fortitude-adjacent virtues or vices to the precepts of fortitude

## Support-Type Discipline

- `explicit_textual` is used for direct tract statements such as patience being a part of fortitude or the gift of fortitude being reckoned a gift
- `strong_textual_inference` is used when the passage strongly supports a linkage but does not state it in a compact formula
- `structural_editorial` remains confined to treatment correspondences and synthesis framing

## Unresolved Or Ambiguous Points

- `q.136` still needs continued human review around the exact relation of longanimity and constancy to patience
- `q.137` remains the clearest under-annotated question in the closure block
- `q.139` needs continued scrutiny where gift-of-fortitude material touches broader fortitude synthesis
- `q.140` needs continued scrutiny because precept-linkage edges are easy to overextend beyond the local question
