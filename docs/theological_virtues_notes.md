# Theological Virtues Notes

## Scope

This reviewed block is intentionally limited to:

- `II-II, qq. 1–16` faith
- `II-II, qq. 17–22` hope
- `II-II, qq. 23–46` charity

## Normalization Decisions

- `love` is not automatically `charity`.
- `Love (Act)` is kept distinct from:
  - `Charity`
  - `Love (Passion)`
- `Despair (Sin)` is kept distinct from despair as a passion.
- `Hatred (Sin)` is kept distinct from hatred as a passion.
- `Mercy`, `peace`, and `joy` are handled conservatively as tract-level act/effect nodes in this block rather than being flattened into a single generic moral-affect bucket.
- `Ecclesial Unity` is kept distinct from generic peace so that `schism` can be represented more precisely.

## Support Classification Notes

- `explicit_textual` is used where Aquinas directly states the relation:
  - `heresy species_of unbelief`
  - `apostasy species_of unbelief`
  - `blasphemy opposed_by confession_of_faith`
  - `wisdom_gift corresponds_to beatitude_peacemakers`
- `strong_textual_inference` is used sparingly where the tract strongly supports the relation without a formulaic naming:
  - `charity perfected_by wisdom_gift`
  - `folly opposed_by wisdom_gift`
- `structural_editorial` is reserved for non-doctrinal tract framing and reviewed navigational correspondences. It does not appear in default doctrinal graph views.

## Unresolved Or Deliberately Deferred Mappings

- `hope` and the `gift of fear` remain closely related in tract structure, but this block avoids overstating the exact reviewed doctrinal edge unless the passage is direct enough.
- `sin against the Holy Spirit` has multiple senses in `q.14`; reviewed doctrinal use stays narrow and avoids pretending the tract has one uncontested modern schema.
- `war` remains in the tract because Aquinas treats it there, but reviewed doctrinal export avoids flattening every war reference into a simple contrary-to-charity edge.
- `envy` in `q.14` as a species against the Spirit is not silently collapsed into the broader envy of `q.36`.
