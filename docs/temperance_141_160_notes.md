# Temperance 141-160 Notes

## Scope

This note covers the reviewed temperance phase-1 block:

- `II-II, qq. 141–160`

The goal of this pass is a strong tract backbone, not closure on every later temperance distinction in `qq. 161–170`.

## Normalization Decisions

- `temperance` is kept as the cardinal-virtue node for this tract and is not flattened into generic self-control language.
- `abstinence` and `fasting` remain distinct:
  - `abstinence` is treated as the virtue-level or part-level concept
  - `fasting` is treated as a tract-local practice or act and currently uses `act_of` where the text supports that relation
- `sobriety` remains distinct from `abstinence`.
- `gluttony` remains distinct from `drunkenness`.
- `chastity` remains distinct from `virginity`.
- `lust` remains distinct from its tract-local parts in `q.154`.
- `continence` remains distinct from `temperance`.
- `meekness` remains distinct from `clemency`.
- `modesty_general` remains distinct from later humility / studiousness / exterior-modesty material.
- `anger` is tracked carefully because passages in this tract can press either:
  - passion-level `concept.anger`
  - vice-level `concept.anger_vice`

## Part-Taxonomy Decisions

`q.143` is treated as the controlling taxonomy question for this block.

Current reviewed placement:

- integral parts:
  - `shamefacedness`
  - `honesty_temperance`
- subjective parts:
  - `abstinence`
  - `sobriety`
  - `chastity`
  - `purity_temperance`
- potential parts:
  - `continence`
  - `meekness`
  - `modesty_general`

Important non-decisions:

- `fasting` is not auto-promoted to a subjective part
- `virginity` is not auto-promoted to a subjective part
- `clemency` is not auto-promoted to a potential part solely because it appears beside meekness in `q.157`

## Matter-Domain Decisions

The tract uses explicit domain distinctions in reviewed exports:

- food:
  - `abstinence`
  - `fasting`
  - `gluttony`
- drink:
  - `sobriety`
  - `drunkenness`
- sexual pleasure:
  - `chastity`
  - `virginity`
  - `lust`
  - tract-local parts of lust
- anger-related:
  - `meekness`
  - `anger`
  - `anger_vice`
  - `incontinence` where the tract support turns on anger-matter
- outward moderation:
  - `modesty_general`

`q.157` is handled as a mixed anger-domain question with distinct concepts for meekness and clemency.

## Support-Type Decisions

- `explicit_textual`
  - used where the tract clearly names a relation, part placement, or opposition
- `strong_textual_inference`
  - used where the local passage strongly supports a doctrinal claim without a formulaic statement
- `structural_editorial`
  - used for treatment correspondences, tract framing, and synthesis relations that should remain available but not appear in doctrinal-only views by default
